## The Author

Rabbi Shlomo Elyashiv (1841–1926), known as the **Baal haLeshem**, was one of the foremost Kabbalists of the modern era. Born in Lithuania, he devoted his life to the systematic study and exposition of the *Lurianic Kabbalah* — the mystical tradition as taught by Rabbi Isaac Luria (the Arizal, 1534–1572) and transcribed by his chief disciple, Rabbi Chaim Vital.

His magnum opus, *Leshem Shvo v'Achlama* — named after three of the twelve precious stones on the High Priest's breastplate — is widely regarded as the most comprehensive and rigorous presentation of Kabbalistic metaphysics ever written.

## The Work

The book is structured as a series of **Shaarim** (Gates), each probing a foundational question in Kabbalistic cosmology: the nature of the Divine Names, the *tzimtzum* (Divine contraction), the structure of the spiritual worlds, and the relationship between the infinite and the finite.
