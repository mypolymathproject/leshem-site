# *Leshem Shvo v'Achlama* — An Annotated Translation

**ספר לשם שבו ואחלמה – הקדמות ושערים**

---

## The Author

Rabbi Shlomo Elyashiv (1841–1926), known as the **Baal haLeshem**, was one of the great Kabbalists of the modern era. His magnum opus, *Leshem Shvo v'Achlama* ("Jacinth, Agate and Amethyst" — three of the twelve stones on the High Priest's breastplate), is a comprehensive systematic exposition of the Lurianic Kabbalah — the mystical tradition as taught by Rabbi Isaac Luria (the Arizal, 1534–1572).

## The Work

*Leshem Shvo v'Achlama* consists of introductions and "Gates" (*Shaarim*), each exploring a fundamental aspect of Kabbalistic cosmology. This translation covers the **Introductions and Gates** (*Hekdamot v'Shearim*).

The text follows a distinct pattern:

- **Hebrew source text** — the Baal haLeshem's own words
- **English translation** (in bold) — a close rendering of the Hebrew
- *Commentary* — explanatory notes to aid the reader unfamiliar with Kabbalistic terminology
