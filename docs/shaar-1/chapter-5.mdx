***Shaar* 1 Chapter 5**

**The Revelation via the Name *Havaye* and via other Names**

<Passage
  marker="§ 5:1"

/>

<Passage

  translation="Now even if we depict some letter in thought alone, its entire shape and image is elicited only by virtue of the shape and structure of their physical and material existence."

<Passage

  translation="And particularly so since our power of thought, and the ability to form a mental image, is linked with the physical and material.[^2] It is impossible for our mind's eye to imagine in any other way something spiritual devoid of the physical, because our entire existence is built exclusively upon the physical and material."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 5:6"
  hebrew="ועכ״פ הוּא כִּי כָּל צוּרַת הַשֵּׁמוֹת כֻּלָּם הַמֻּשָּׂגִים אֵלֵינוּ הִנֵּה הוּא רַק ע״י בִּנְיַן צוּרָתָם שֶׁבְּנוּיִם וַעֲשׂוּיִם מִגֶּשֶׁם וְחֹמֶר שֶׁהוּא מִדְיוֹ ע״ג קְלָף וּמְצֻיָּרִים מִתְּמוּנַת אוֹתִיּוֹת ע״י כְּתָב וְסוֹפֵר"
  translation="In any event, the form of all the divine names – all those we can comprehend – is achieved by means of constructing their form on the basis of their physical and material structure as ink on parchment. And they are fashioned in the mind's eye from the image of the letters written by a scribe on parchment."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 5:7"
  hebrew="וכ״ז הֲרֵי נוֹדַע וּפָשׁוּט הוּא שְׂאוֹר קְדֻשָּׁתוֹ ית״ש הוּא רָחוֹק וּמוּשְׁלָּל מִלִּתְפֹּס אוֹתוֹ בִּמְצִיאוּת כְּזֶה בְּתַכְלִית הַשְּׁלִילָה וְהַהֶרְחֵק"
  translation="It is known and even obvious that the light of His blessed holiness is far removed from, and cannot in any way at all be grasped – even remotely – in an empirical manner."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 5:8"
  hebrew="וְהָאוֹת עַל זֶה כִּי הֲרֵי גַּם אַחַר רוֹמְמוֹת מַעֲלַת קְדּוּשַׁת הָאוֹתִיּוֹת דְּהַשֵּׁם הוי״ה ב״ה וְכֵן כָּל הַשֵּׁמוֹת כֻּלָּם הִנֵּה אֵין לָהֶם קְדוּשָׁה כְּלָל אֶלָּא רַק כְּשֶׁנִּכְתְּבוּ לִשְׁמָן דַּוְקָא"
  translation="The proof of this is that even given the lofty, holy status of the letters of the blessed Name Havaye, and all of the other names as well, they have no holiness at all unless they were written *lishman* – for their own sake."
>

Torah law requires that Torah scrolls, *tefillin* and *mezuzot* must be written *lishma* – for the intention of fulfilling the *mitzvah* – and not for some ulterior motive, or even without any intention. If that condition is not filled they are invalid.[^3]

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 5:9"
  hebrew="וְאָמְרוּ בְּפֵרוּשׁ גִּיטִין נ״ד ע״ב שֶׁכָּל סֵפֶר תּוֹרָה שֶׁאֵין אַזְכָּרוֹת שֶׁלּוֹ כְּתוּבוֹת לִשְׁמָן אֵינוֹ שָׁוֶה כְּלוּם"
  translation="Our sages said explicitly (*Gittin* 54b) that any Torah scroll whose holy names were not written for their own sake is not worth anything."
>

Note that the words "not *worth* anything" is the actual expression used in the Talmud since the discussion there is whether a scribe who wrote a *Sefer Torah*, but did not write the names *lishmo* (with the proper intention), can charge his client anything. According to Rav Ami the scroll is worthless and he loses his scribal fee. The *Shulchan Aruch, Yoreh De'ah* 274:1 and commentaries describe the scroll as *invalid* (*possul*) if written without proper intention.

</Passage>

  translation="This is all because the actual letters in the form they are before us in a Torah scroll, or *tefillin* and *mezuzot*, are made and formed from physical and material things – ink on parchment – and therefore there is no Divine holiness at all in their existence in and of themselves; only by virtue of the extended radiance of His light to them."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 5:11"

  translation="For His light extends to them and suffuses them when they are written for their own sake, and when all the other pre-conditions specified in *halacha* (Jewish law) are complied with. And indeed then the blessed light of His holiness is in them in the letters themselves; they are permeated with Divine holiness."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 5:12"
  hebrew="וּבִפְרָט הַשֵּׁם הוי״ה ב״ה שֶׁנִּקְרָא שֵׁם הָעֱצֶם מִשּׁוּם שֶׁעַצְמוּתוֹ גּוּפָהּ נִתְגַּלָּה בְּהַשֵּׁם הַזֶּה ע״י הָאֲצִילוּת וְגִלּוּיוֹ בּוֹ הוּא בְּלִי שׁוּם לְבוּשׁ כְּלָל"
  translation="This is particularly so regarding the blessed Name Havaye, which is called 'the Essential Name' since His very Essence is revealed in this Name by way of the world of Atzilut. Moreover, His revelation within it – within the Name Havaye – is with no 'garment' at all,"
>

A 'garment' (*levush*) in this context is something that screens and filters the revelation in order to keep it discreet. The *Zohar*[^4] writes that the Torah itself is a prime example of this in that some of the deepest wisdom of the Torah – the Torah as it is in the world of Atzilut – is clothed in the 'garments' of stories and seemingly mundane events.

</Passage>

  translation="since they were revealed to us only existentially, but not essentially, as in the first concept explained above in chapter 4."

[^1]: Note that these terms, *metziut* (existence) and *mehut* (essence) will be used extensively in the following chapters.

[^2]: *Meir HaLeshem* refers here to Rambam's *Pirush HaMishnah* (*Sanhedrin* 10:1) where he notes that just as a person born blind cannot comprehend color, or a person born deaf the sound of voices, so too the body cannot comprehend the things that delight the soul.

[^3]: See *Mishneh Torah, Tefillin, Metzuzot v'Sefer Torah* 1:15.

[^4]: *Zohar, Behaalotecha* 152a.

[^5]: See *Zohar* II pp. 90b, 124a that the entire Torah is one single name of the Holy One, blessed be He (*Meir HaLeshem*). See also GRA's commentary to *Sefer Yetzirah* 1:1.

[^6]: Literally, 'devoid of comprehension.'

[^7]: *Meir HaLeshem* notes that the Baal HaLeshem writes elsewhere that Kabbalistic matters are of the world of Atzilut and beyond, and for that reason Kabbalah is called *nistar* – hidden wisdom – since it transcends the comprehension of created beings. This is not so with *niglah* – the revealed aspects of Torah – which are accessible to the human mind.
