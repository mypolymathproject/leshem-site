**שער א פרק ב**

***Sha'ar*** **1 Chapter 2**

**The Revelation of the Name *Havaye* in *Atzilut* and in *Biy"A***

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:1"
  hebrew="וְהִנֵּה כָּל הַגִּלּוּיִם הַלָּלוּ אֲשֶׁר נִתְגַּלָּה אֵלֵינוּ בְּכָל זֶה הִנֵּה הוּא רַק עַל יְדֵי שֶׁהִמְצִיא אֶת כָּל הַמְּצִיאוּת"
  translation="Now all of these revelations mentioned above that were radiated to us are nevertheless only because He brought forth all of existence."
>

It is by way of His bringing the worlds into existence that we have any grasp at all of Godliness. Indeed, according to the *Zohar*[^9] and the Arizal,[^10] this was in fact the purpose of creation.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:2"
  hebrew="אֲבָל זוּלַת הַמְּצִיאוּת הִנֵּה הוּא פָּשׁוּט בְּאַחְדוּת פְּשׁוּטָה בְּתַכְלִית הַפְּשִׁיטוּת"
  translation="In Himself, other than in terms of this existence that He brought into being, He is 'simple' with the simple unity of absolute simplicity."
>

The term 'simple' here requires explanation. The closest conceptual equivalents may be 'essential', 'primordial', 'elemental' or perhaps even 'uncompounded' — in the sense of an absolute infinite oneness not comprised of disparate, limited parts.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:3"
  hebrew="וְאֵין לוֹ שׁוּם גִּלּוּי בְּשׁוּם שֵׁם וְתֹאַר כְּלָל וְאֵין בּוֹ שׁוּם תְּפִיסָה כְּלָל בְּשׁוּם דִּבּוּר וְשֵׁם וּמִלָּה וּבְשׁוּם אוֹת וּנְקֻדָּה"
  translation="He in His essence is not revealed in any Divine Name or description and He cannot be described at all in speech or name or word, and not in any letter or vowel point."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:4"
  hebrew="כִּי הֲרֵי כּוּלָּם הֵם בְּהוֹרָאַת גְּבוּלִי עַל כָּל פָּנִים וּבְאַחְדוּתוֹ הַפְּשׁוּטָה הִנֵּה הוּא מְשֻׁלָּל מִזֶּה בְּתַכְלִית הַשְּׁלִילָה"
  translation="For they — words, names, descriptors — are all indicative of limitation in some sense, and in His uncompounded Oneness all descriptors are completely negated in regard to Him."
>

Any descriptor must have some limitation in order to describe the object of the description. Since He is in Himself infinite, and an indivisible unity, it is not possible to describe Him as He is in Himself. All descriptive terms are thus completely negated.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:5"
  hebrew="וַהֲרֵי הֲוָה אֲמִתַּת פְּשִׁיטוּתוֹ נֶעֱלַם בְּתַכְלִית הַהֶעֱלֶם וְאִי אֶפְשָׁר לְדַבֵּר וּלְהַרְהֵר בְּעִנְיַן אִמְּתַתּוֹ וּשְׁלֵמוּתוֹ אֲשֶׁר זוּלַת הַמְּצִיאוּת"
  translation="Thus His true essential being is completely concealed and it is impossible to speak and think about the truth and completeness of His being which is devoid of existence in the regular sense of 'existence.'"
>

As the Baal haLeshem just explained, our categories of thought and speech are inadequate to the task of positive descriptions of the Being whose existence transcends all limitations. However, as we will see, we can *negate* or repudiate any descriptions which are part of common parlance, and in this way come to a certain level of understanding, albeit negative understanding — i.e. what He isn't (but not what He is).[^11]

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:6"
  hebrew="אֶלָּא רַק בְּדֶרֶךְ שְׁלִילָה לְבַד הֲיְינוּ לִשְׁלֹל מֵאֲמִתַּת עַצְמוּתוֹ וּפְשִׁיטוּתוֹ כָּל עִנְיָן גְּבוּל וּתְמוּנָה וְדִמְיוֹן"
  translation="But we can make use of the method of negation exclusively — that is to say, negate or repudiate any concept of limitation, representation or likeness from His true and simple essence."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:7"
  hebrew="וְרוֹצֶה לוֹמַר כִּי הִנֵּה כָּל גִּלּוּיָיו הִנֵּה הֵם בְּעֶרֶךְ פְּשִׁיטוֹתוֹ אֲשֶׁר לְמַעְלָה מֵהֶם בִּבְחִינַת כֹּחַ גְּבוּלִי וְהֻתַּר לְתָאֵר בָּהֶם כָּל תָּאֳרֵי דְּשִׁעוּרֵי קוֹמָה"
  translation="Meaning to say, all revelations that emerge from Him, when compared with the simplicity of His essence that transcends them, are to be regarded as finite powers which it is permissible to describe using any of the descriptive terms that apply to the shiur koma."
>

The term *shiur koma* refers to those finite spiritual worlds that can in be in some sense described or 'computed,' as explained in Rabbi Yishmael's *Pirkei Heichalot.* Since these are all 'revelations that emerge from Him,' in the words of the Baal haLeshem, and are not His essence, it is permissible to use descriptive terms, and even anthropomorphisms to describe them, as the Baal haLeshem will show. Kabbalah often uses anthropomorphic terms when describing the worlds 'outside of' His essence.[^12]

</Passage>

>

The *tzimtzum* is a major subject that requires some explanation.[^15]

As the Baal haLeshem will explain later, this very first radiance or revelation after the *tzimtzum* is called the *kav*[^16] — the ray of light (so to speak) that after a series of further reductions and descents becomes the first plane of existence known in Kabbalah as the meta-world *Adam Kadmon*, the highest and most exalted of the five worlds. *Adam* means "in the likeness of" or "in the image of," from the Hebrew word *domeh*. *Kadmon* means "primordial" or "primary," from the Hebrew root *kadam*. So, *Adam Kadmon* is the primordial, transcendent world which is "in the likeness of" the Infinite Light which preceded it and which became concealed in the process of creation. This means that even though *Adam Kadmon* is a world that comes into being through the concealment of the Infinite Light, it is such an elevated plane of reality that it remains transcendent — "in the likeness of" the Infinite Light. Thus, the world of *Adam Kadmon* could be called a meta-world, a level so sublime, pure and transcendent that it is almost imperceptible. It cleaves to and mirrors the original Infinite Light.

</Passage>

<Passage

*[Meaning to say, this is the Name with which He emerged from concealment to revelation, and His Essence was revealed in this Name. And when it says 'with Myself' this refers to the radiance in Atzilut that was revealed there from the concealed True Being above].*

Note that the square brackets are in the original. The meaning of 'stipulations with Myself' will be explained shortly.

The World of *Atzilut* (emanation) is the first of the immanent worlds. The word *Atzilut* in Hebrew derives from the word *etzel*, meaning "close to" or "near."[^19] The world of *Atzilut* is "close to" its source, even though it is not united and identified with it to the same degree as the world of *Adam Kadmon*. Nevertheless it is a lofty and rarefied plane of reality which is merely emanated, not yet created. But the word *Atzilut* also means "to set aside," "separated from," as in the verse "And I will set aside from (*v'atzalti*) the spirit that is upon you and place it upon them"[^20] (Numbers 11:17), for the world of *Atzilut* is, so to speak, 'set aside from' God's essence, as the Baal haLeshem goes on to explain.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 2:26"
  hebrew="הוּא שְׁמִי שֶׁהִתְנֵיתִי בֵּינִי לְבֵין בְּרִיּוֹתַי וְרוֹצֶה לוֹמַר שֶׁנִּתְגַּלָּה עַל יָדוֹ לְזוּלָתוֹ וְהוּא בְּעוֹלָמוֹת בְּרִיאָה יְצִירָה וַעֲשִׂיָּה"
  translation="'And this is the Name with which I made stipulations between Myself and My creations,' meaning to say that there was revelation to others — i.e. to those who are not Him — by way of the Name. This alludes to the worlds of Beriah, Yetzirah and Asiyah."
>

*Beriah*, the world of Creation, is the second immanent world. It is a tremendous step down from the world of *Atzilut*, as it is the beginning of created being. ברא — the root of the word *Beriah* ('creation'), is always used in Hebrew to signify *yesh mi-ayin* — creation *ex nihilo*.[^21] This implies the coming into being of limited, restricted, finite existence.

*Yetzirah* is the third of the four immanent spiritual worlds, the realm of spiritual existence in which the limited nature of the created beings takes on form and definition, and *Asiyah*, the fourth and lowest immanent world, is the dimension where Godliness is most concealed. It is the final stage in the creative process and the lowest aspect of it includes the physical universe. The Baal haLeshem will explain all of these in their proper place (in *Shaar* 2).

</Passage>

  translation="In addition, the revelation to 'something other' — i.e. to those levels below Atzilut that are regarded as 'other' — that is drawn down from the world of Atzilut to the worlds of Beriah, Yetzirah and Asiyah (abbreviated as BiYA), is only drawn from the aspects of zeir anpin and malchut. >

*Ze'ir anpin* is a cluster of *sefirot* that includes *chesed, gevurah, tiferet, netzach, hod* and *yesod.* Its composition and function will be discussed later on. *Malchut* is the final *sefirah* and is also referred to as *Shechinah*. These will be explained at length in *shaar* 7.
