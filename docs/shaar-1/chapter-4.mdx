**שער א פרק ד**

***Shaar* 1 Chapter 4**

**Two Aspects of Revelation in Atzilut**

<Passage
  marker="§ 4:1"
  hebrew="וְהִנֵּה הָיָה סֵדֶר גִּלּוּיָיו אֲשֶׁר בַּאֲצִילוּת מְשֻׁעָר וּמְכֻוָּן בְּכָל עִנְיָנֵיהֶם בְּמִדָּה וּבְמִשְׁקָל לְפִי סֵדֶר תּוֹצְאוֹת הבי״ע שֶׁרָצָה לְהוֹצִיא עַל יְדֵיהֶם"
  translation="Now, the order of His revelations in the world of Atzilut were measured and precise in all aspects, measured and weighed, so to speak, according to the order of the results that He wished to produce thereby in the worlds of *BiYA*."
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 4:2"
  hebrew="כִּי הִנֵּה כָּל אוֹתָן הַגִּלּוּיִם אֲשֶׁר בַּאֲצִילוּת הִנֵּה הֵם עֲצְמָם הַשָּׁרָשִׁים הָעִקָּרִיִּים דָּהָעוֹלָמוֹת בי״ע וְכָל עִנְיָנֵיהֶם בִּכְלָלוּת וּפְרָטוּת"
  translation="Because all of those revelations in Atzilut are themselves the primary roots of the worlds of *BiYA* and all related concepts both generally and specifically."
>

In Talmudic dialectic when a general rule is followed by an explicit particular, the rule is limited to the specified particular. The Baal HaLeshem invokes this exegetical rule here as well, since the general revelation in Atzilut is limited to the specified particulars He desired in the lower worlds of *Beriah, Yetzirah* and *Asiyah*, as explained in chapters 2 & 3.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 4:3"
  hebrew="וְנוֹדַע הוּא כִּי בְּכָל שֹׁרֶשׁ הִנֵּה מְסֻדָּר בּוֹ כָּל כְּחוֹתָיו וּתְכוּנוֹתָיו רַק לְפִי עִנְיָנֵי תּוֹלְדוֹת הֶעָנָף וְהַפְּרִי שֶׁנִּמְשַׁךְ וְיוֹצֵא מִמֶּנּוּ"
  translation="It is known that in every root of a plant all the capacities and qualities are arrayed only according to the products that will emerge later in the branch and fruit that derive and emerge from them – from the roots."
>

The analogy here to a plant and its roots and fruits comes to illustrate an important principle the Baal HaLeshem outlined earlier – that the products in the lower world (ultimately the 'fruits') are the primary purpose, not the roots. The roots serve merely to nourish and support the plant and fruits that grow from them.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 4:7"
  hebrew="וְהוּא מִשּׁוּם שֶׁשּׁוֹרֶשׁ כָּל מְצִיאוּתָם אֲשֶׁר בְּכָל הֳעוֹלָמוֹת בי״ע בִּכְלָל וּבִּפְרָט וְכָל סִדְרָם וְתַהֲלוּכוֹתֵיהֶם הֵן מָה שֶׁנּוֹגֵעַ לְעֲצְמוּתָם הָעִקָּרִי וְהֵן כָּל מִקְרֵיהֶם שֶׁנַּעֲשָׂה בָּהֶם"
  translation="This is because the root of the existence of all entities in all of the worlds of *BiYA*, in general and specifically, and the way they are all arranged in their various groupings and the manner in which they proceed – whether relating directly to their essence or whether to tangential occurrences that involve them –"
/>

<Passage
  marker="§ 4:8"
  hebrew="הִנֵּה שֹׁרֶשׁ כָּל הַדְּבָרִים הוּא הַכָּל בַּאֲצִילוּת וּמִשָּׁם נִמְשַׁךְ בָּהֶם וְהֵם הֵם גִּלּוּיָיו ית״ש אֲשֶׁר בְּאֲצִילוּת שֶׁבָּהֶם הוּא מְהַוֶּה הַכָּל וּמַנְהִיג אֶת הַכָּל תָּמִיד וְהֵם הֵם שַׁמּוֹתָיו ית״ש"
  translation="– the root of all of these things is all in Atzilut, from whence it is drawn down to them. These are His very revelations, may His Name be blessed, in the world of Atzilut, with which He brings everything into existence, and by which He directs everything constantly. And these revelations are themselves His names, may His Name be blessed."
>

The *Meir HaLeshem*[^15] notes that the author seems to imply here that the primary emphasis in Kabbalah study should be knowledge of the holy names and the manner in which they are revealed.[^16] However, another authority, Rabbi Yitzchak Isaac Chaver[^17] (1789-1853), states that the primary goal is to understand Divine Providence (השגחה פרטית) and its workings. Nevertheless, *Meir HaLeshem* opines, there may not be a difference of opinion, since the workings of Divine Providence IS in fact via the revelation of His names.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 4:9"
  hebrew="שֶׁהֵם הַשָּׁרַשִׁים הָרִאשׁוֹנִים וְהַכֹּחוֹת הַפְּנִימִים שֶׁל כָּל הַבְּרִיאָה כֻּלָּהּ וְכָל צֶאֱצָאֶיהָ בְּכָל מָה שֶׁנַּעֲשָׂה בָּהּ בְּכָל עֵת שֶׁהַכָּל הוּא מִמֶּנּוּ"
  translation="These revelations are the first roots and the inner capacities of the entire creation and all of its progeny – everything that is produced thereby at any time – it is all from Him."
>

The term 'inner capacities' or 'powers' refers to particular, immanent divine revelations that are manifested in specific, limited and definable functions (sometimes called 'vessels' – *kelim*) indicated by the various Divine names. These are the opposite of 'encompassing' or transcendent revelations (*makifim*) that are not confined to specific, limited functions.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 4:14"
  hebrew="הִנֵּה עכ״ז אֵין לָנוּ וְלֹא לְשׁוּם נִבְרָא שׁוּם תְּפִיסָה וְהֲשָּׂגָה בַּמְּהוּת אֵיזֶה גִּלּוּי מִכָּל אוֹתָן הַגִּלּוּיָם כֻּלָּם"
  translation="With all that being said, neither we nor any other created being has any grasp or comprehension of the essence of any of those revelations."
>

The Baal HaLeshem makes a clear distinction here between knowledge of the *existence* (מציאות) of divine revelations, and comprehending their *essence* (מהות) which is not possible for us. This hearkens back to Shaar 1 chap 2 where he explained the difference between positive descriptions and the *negation* or repudiation of any descriptions. See also *Moreh Nevuchin* cited in chapter 2 footnote 12. *Meir HaLeshem* notes that *existence* (מציאות) refers to revelations in the worlds of *BiYA*.[^20]

</Passage>

The *Mishna* in *Avot* 5:1 declares that God created the world with 10 utterances. The *Sefer Yetzirah* (2:4-5) describes this process and mentions that the most basic combinations of letters of the Hebrew Alphabet, i.e. two-letter combinations, form 231 pairs of letters that were used in the construction of the universe. They are called the 231 'gates'.
