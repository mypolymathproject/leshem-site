**שער א פרק ב**

***Sha’ar*** **1 chapter 2**

**The Revelation of the Name Havaye in *Atzilut* and in *Biy”A***

וְהִנֵּה כָּל הַגִּלּוּיִם הַלָּלוּ אֲשֶׁר נִתְגַּלָּה אֵלֵינוּ בְּכָל זֶה הִנֵּה הוּא רַק ע"י שֶׁהִמְצִיא אֶת כָּל הַמְּצִיאוּת 

**Now all of these revelations**[^9] mentioned above **that were radiated to us are nevertheless only because He brought forth all of existence.**

It is by way of His bringing the worlds into existence that we have any grasp at all of Godliness. Indeed, according to the *Zohar*[^10] and the Arizal[^11], this was in fact the purpose of creation.

אֲבָל זוּלַת הַמְּצִיאוּת הִנֵּה הוּא פָּשׁוּט בְּאַחְדוּת פְּשׁוּטָה בְּתַכְלִית הַפְּשִׁיטוּת 

In Himself, **other than** in terms of **this existence** that He brought into being **He is ‘simple’** **with the simple unity** **of absolute** **simplicity.**

The term ‘simple’ here requires explanation. The closest conceptual equivalents may be ‘essential’, ‘primordial’, ‘elemental’ or perhaps even ‘uncompounded’ – in the sense of an absolute infinite oneness not comprised of disparate, limited parts.

וְאֵין לוֹ שׁוּם גִּלּוּי בְּשׁוּם שֵׁם וְתֹאַר כְּלָל וְאֵין בּוֹ שׁוּם תְּפִיסָה כְּלָל בְּשׁוּם דִּבּוּר וְשֵׁם וּמִלָּה וּבְשׁוּם אוֹת וּנְקֻדָּה

**He** in His essence **is not revealed in any** Divine **Name or description and He cannot be described at all in speech or name or word, and not in any letter or** vowel **point.**

כִּי הֲרֵי כֻּלָּם הֵם בְּהוֹרָאַת גְּבוּלִי עכ"פ \[=עַל כָּל פָּנִים\] וּבְאַחְדוּתוֹ הַפְּשׁוּטָה הִנֵּה הוּא מְשֻׁלָּל מִזֶּה בְּתַכְלִית הַשְּׁלִילָה

**For they** – words, names, descriptors – **are all indicative of limitation in some sense, and in His uncompounded Oneness** all descriptors **are completely negated** in regard to **Him.**

Any descriptor must have some limitation in order to describe the object of the description. Since He is in Himself infinite, and an indivisible unity, it is not possible to describe Him as He is in Himself. All descriptive terms are thus completely negated.

וַהֲרֵי הֲוָה אֲמִתַּת פְּשִׁיטוּתוֹ נֶעֱלַם בְּתַכְלִית הַהֶעֱלֶם וְאִי אֶפְשָׁר לְדַבֵּר וּלְהַרְהֵר בְּעִנְיַן אִמְּתַתּוֹ וּשְׁלֵמוּתוֹ אֲשֶׁר זוּלַת הַמְּצִיאוּת

**Thus His true essential being is completely concealed and it is impossible to speak and think about the truth and completeness** of His being **which is** **devoid of existence** in the regular sense of ‘existence.’

As the Baal haLeshem just explained, our categories of thought and speech are inadequate to the task of positive descriptions of the Being whose existence transcends all limitations. However, as we will see, we can *negate* or repudiate any descriptions which are part of common parlance, and in this way come to a certain level of understanding, albeit negative understanding – i.e. what He isn’t (but not what He is).[^12]  

אֶלָּא רַק בְּדֶרֶךְ שְׁלִילָה לְבַד הֲיְינוּ לִשְׁלֹל מֵאֲמִתַּת עַצְמוּתוֹ וּפְשִׁיטוּתוֹ כָּל עִנְיָן גְּבוּל וּתְמוּנָה וְדִמְיוֹן 

**But** we can make use of **the method of negation exclusively – that is to say, negate** or repudiate **any concept of limitation, representation or likeness from His true and simple essence.**

ור"ל \[וְרוֹצֶה לוֹמַר\] כִּי הִנֵּה כָּל גִּלּוּיָיו הִנֵה הֵם בְּעֶרֶךְ פְּשִׁיטוֹתוֹ אֲשֶׁר לְמַעְלָה מֵהֶם בבחי' \[בִּבְחִינַת\] כֹּחַ גְּבוּלִי וְהֻתַּר לְתָאֵר בָּהֶם כָּל תָּאֳרִי דְּשִׁעוּרֵי קוֹמָה 

**Meaning to say, all revelations** that emerge **from Him, when compared with the simplicity** of His essence **that transcends them, are** to be regarded as **finite powers which it is permissible to describe using any of the descriptive terms that apply to the *‘shiur koma*’.**

The term *shiur koma* refers to those finite spiritual worlds that can in be in some sense described or ‘computed,’ as explained in Rabbi Yishmael’s *Pirkei Heichalot.* Since these are all ‘revelations that emerge from Him,’ in the words of the Baal haLeshem, and are not His essence, it is permissible to use descriptive terms, and even anthropomorphisms to describe them, as the Baal haLeshem will show. Kabbalah often uses anthropomorphic terms when describing the worlds ‘outside of’ His essence.[^13] 

וּכְמוֹ שֶׁנִּמְצָא בַּתּוֹרָה כַּמָּה תּוֹאֲרִים בְּזֶה כְּמוֹ וְתַחַת רַגְלָיו, כְּתוּבִים בְּאֶצְבַּע אֱלֹהִים וְכַדּוֹמֶה הַרְבֵּה וּבִפְרָט בְּשִׁיר הַשִּׁירִים וּבְדִבְרֵי חַכְמֵי הָאֱמֶת 

**As we find in the Torah** itself **a number of such descriptive terms, such as “Underneath His feet…”**[^14]**; “Written by the finger of God”**[^15] **and many similar expressions, particularly in The Song of Songs** (*Shir HaShirim*) **and in the writings of the ‘Sages of Truth’** – the Kabbalists.

הַגַּם שֶׁעִקָּרָם הֵם בֶּאֱמֶת רַק עַל שֵׁם הַמְּצִיאוּת שֶׁיָּצָא מֵהֶם עכ"ז הֲרֵי הֻתַּר לְתָאֵר אוֹתָם גַּם בְּגִלּוּיָיו עַצְמָן ג"כ \[גַּם כֵּן\] עכ"פ \[עַל כָּל פָּנִים\] מִשּׁוּם שֶׁהֵם הַשֹּׁרֶשׁ לְהַמְּצִיאוּת

**Even though primarily these** descriptive terms **truly only apply to the** limited **existence that emerges from them, nevertheless it is permissible to use these descriptions for the revelations themselves as well, since they are (at the very least) the origin of** limited **existence.**

משׁא"כ בִּפְשִׁיטוּתוֹ הָאֲמִתִּית אֲשֶׁר לְמַעְלָה מִגִּלּוּיָיו הִנֵּה שָׁם אָסוּר בְּכָל חֹמֶר הָאִסּוּר הַיּוֹתֵר אֶפְשָׁרִי לְתָאֵר שָׁם ח"ו שׁוּם בְּחִי' הַמּוֹרֶה עַל אֵיזֶה כֹּחַ גְּבוּלִי וּתְמוּנָה וְדִמְּיוֹן

**But this is not the case regarding His true simple** i.e. essential being – the ‘Luminary’ so to speak – **that transcends all the revelations**  

וּמְשֻׁלַּל שָׁם מכ"ז \[מִכָּל זֶה\] בְּתַכְלִית הַשְּׁלִילָה וְאִי אֶפְשָׁר לְהִתְבּוֹנֵן שָׁם כְּלָל אֶלָּא רַק בְּדֶרֶךְ שְׁלִילָה לְבַד

From which all descriptive terms **are absolutely and totally negated. It is impossible to** even **ponder that level other than exclusively by way of negation –**

It is possible to contemplate the transcendent Essence only in a negative sense – by repudiating any and all descriptive terms, in the manner that the Baal haLeshem will describe shortly.

לִשְׁלֹל מִשָּׁם כָּל עִנְיָן הַמּוֹרֶה אֵיזֶה חֶסְרוֹן לִשְׁלֵמוּת הָאֲמִתִּית וְכָל עִנְיָן הַשָּׂגָה וּתְפִיסָה מִזּוּלָתוֹ 

**– by negating there** in His essence **all concepts that indicate any deficiency in His true completeness** sometimes rendered as the ‘Divine plenum.’

כִּי אֵין בִּלְעֲדוֹ יוֹדֵעַ מְאַמִּתָּתוֹ מְאוּמָה וְכָל מָה שֶׁאָנוּ מְדַבְּרִים מִמֶּנּוּ ית"שׁ הִנֵה הוּא רַק ע"י מָה שֶׁנִּתְגַּלָּה אֵלֵינוּ ע"י הַמְּצִיאוּת 

**For no one besides Him knows anything of His true being; everything we discuss about Him is merely what is revealed to us by way of** the limited **existence** that He brings into being.

Furthermore, this limited existence is all post-*tzimtzum*, where descriptive terms apply, as the Baal haLeshem will explain shortly. 

וְכֵן גַּם הַגִּלּוּי דְּהַשֵּׁם הויה ב"ה הַגַּם שֶׁאָמַרְנוּ שֶׁהוֹרָאָתוֹ הוּא עַל מְצִיאוּתוֹ הָאֲמִתִּית שֶׁהוּא בַּהֹוֶה תָּמִיד וּבִלְתִּי זְמַן 

**So too, the revelation of the blessed Name *Havaye*, even though we stated that it is indicative of His true** infinite and ineffable **existence – in that He is always in the present and beyond time –** 

הִנֵּה גַּם הַגִּלּוּי הַזֶּה נִתְגַּלָּה אֵלֵינוּ בּוֹ ג"כ רַק ע"י הַמְּצִיאוּת דְהָעוֹלָמוֹת 

**even this revelation is disclosed to us via the existence of the worlds.**

כִּי כָּל בְּחִינָה נִמְצָא וְהֹוֶה אֲשֶׁר בְּהוֹרָאַת דְשֵׁם הויה ב"ה הִנֵּה הוּא ג"כ רַק אַחֵר שֶׁנִּתְגַּלָּה בְּהַמְּצִיאוֹת דְהָעוֹלָמוֹת כֻּלָּם 

**Because every aspect indicated by the blessed Name *Havaye* also is to be found** (*nimtza*) **and exists only after it has been revealed within the**

limited, finite **existence of all the worlds.**

כִּי כָּל לָשׁוֹן נִמְצָא הוּא מוֹרֶה עַל גִּלּוּי בְּחִדּוּשׁ שֶׁנִּתְחַדֵּשׁ וְנִתְגַּלָּה וְנִמְצָא 

**Indeed, every instance of the word *nimtza* – ‘is to be found’ – is indicative of a radiance** or revelation **that is generated anew and becomes revealed and is** then **‘to be found.’**

וְכֵן הוּא מוֹרֶה עַל גִּלּוּי לְזוּלָתוֹ כִּי כֵּן הוּא לָשׁוֹן נִמְצָא שֶׁנִּמְצָא לְזוּלָתוֹ 

**In addition,** the word *nimtza* **also denotes a revelation to something** or someone **other** than Himself; **this is actually the semantic definition of ‘*nimtza*’ – ‘to be found by another’** i.e. those who are not Him.

וְעִקַּר לָשׁוֹן נִמְצָא הוּא מִגְּזִירַת יָצָא שֶׁיָּצָא מֵהֶעֱלַמָתוֹ וַהֲרֵי הוּא מוֹרֶה עַל חִדּוּשׁ עכ"פ \[עַל כָּל פָּנִים\]

Indeed, **the primary derivation of the word ‘*nimtza*’ is from ‘*yatza*’** – ‘emerged,’ ‘exited’ – **it emerged from its** prior state of **concealment, which anyway indicates that it is generated anew.**     

ור"ל שֶׁנִּתְחַדֵּשׁ גִּלּוּיוֹ מֵהֶעֱלַמָתוֹ שֶׁבַּתְּחִלָּה כִּי בַּתְּחִלָּה הָיָה נֶעֱלָם וְעַכְשָׁיו נִתְגַּלָּה וְזֶהוּ לָשׁוֹן נִמְצָא 

**Meaning to say that this radiance is a new** revelation **from its prior** state of **concealment; initially it was concealed and now it is revealed. That is the meaning of ‘*nimtza.*’**

וַהֲרֵי הָיָה גִּלּוּיוֹ בְּחִדּוּשׁ וְהֲיְנוּ מֵעֵת הַצִּמְצוּם וּלְהָלְאָה שֶׁאָז יָצָא מֵהֶעֱלַמָתוֹ דִּבְחִי' א"ס \[אֵין סוֹף\] וְנָתַן אֶפְשָׁרִית לְגִלּוּיוֹ

**Thus the radiance is** a **new** phenomenon **– from the moment of the *tzimtzum* and on – for this is when it emerged from its state of concealment** where it was **the aspect of *Ein Sof*** infinite and therefore ineffable. It was the *tzimtzum* that **gave the possibility for it** to emerge **as a revelation.**[^16]

The *tzimtzum* is a major subject that requires some explanation. 

As the Baal haLeshem will explain later, this very first radiance or revelation after the *tzimtzum* is called the *kav*[^17] – the ray of light (so to speak) that after a series of further reductions and descents becomes the first plane of existence known in Kabbalah as the meta-world *Adam Kadmon*, the highest and most exalted of the five worlds. *Adam* means “in the likeness of” or “in the image of,” from the Hebrew word *domeh*. *Kadmon* means “primordial” or “primary,” from the Hebrew root *kadam*. So, *Adam Kadmon* is the primordial, transcendent world which is “in the likeness of” the Infinite Light which preceded it and which became concealed in the process of creation. This means that even though *Adam Kadmon* is a world that comes into being through the concealment of the Infinite Light, it is such an elevated plane of reality that it remains transcendent – “in the likeness of” the Infinite Light (which, by nature, “precedes” the world of *Adam Kadmon*). Thus, the world of *Adam Kadmon* could be called a meta-world, a level so sublime, pure and transcendent that that it is almost imperceptible. It cleaves to and mirrors the original Infinite Light.

וְכֵן נִתְגַּלָּה אָח''כְּ בְּגִלּוּי מַמָּשׁ ע"י הָאֲצִילוּת

**And afterwards this radiance becomes revealed in actuality by way of** the plane of reality called **Atzilut**, the world of Emanation.

וּמֵאָז וָהָלְאָה הוּא נִתְגַּלָּה וְנִמְצָא שֶׁהוּא הֹוֶה תָּמִיד וְהוּא הוֹרָאַת דְשֵׁם הויה ב"ה נִמְצָא קַיָם כַּנַ"ל פ"א 

**And from then on** this Divine radiance **is in a state of revelation and ‘is to be found,’ for He always is** in the present. **This then is what the blessed Name *Havaye* connotes: ‘is to be found’ existing, as explained above chap. 1\.**

אָמְנָם גַּם הוֹרָאַת לָשׁוֹן הֹוֶה הוּא מוֹרֶה ג"כ רַק עַל גִּלּוּי לְזוּלָתוֹ כִּי כָּל שֵׁם וּמִלָּה הִנֵּה הוּא לְעוֹלָם רַק כְּדֵי לְהִשְׁתַּמֵּשׁ עי"ז לְזוּלָתוֹ 

**However, even** the word ***hoveh***[^18] **–** the aspect of the Divine Name that **denotes** **His always being in the present** – **also only implies ‘revelation to another,’** i.e. to those who are not Him, **since every name and word** **is always and only so that another can use it.**

וְהוּא מָה שֶׁאָמְרוּ בַּמְדְבָּר רַבָּה פּ' \[פַּרְשַׁת\] חֻקַּת סִי' ג' אֲנִי ה' הוּא שְׁמִי הוּא שְׁמִי שֶׁקָּרָא לִי אדה"ר \[אָדָם הָרִאשׁוֹן\] הוּא שְׁמִי שֶׁהִתְנֵיתִי בֵּינִי לְבֵין עַצְמִי 

**This is** the significance **of the statement in** Midrash ***Bamidbar Rabba* (*Chukat*** 19:**3\)** on the verse **“I am Havaye** י-ה-ו-ה **that is My Name”**[^19] (*Isaiah* 42:8): “**This is the Name that Adam called me; this is My Name regarding which I made stipulations with Myself”** regarding revelation of Myself therein.

\[ר"ל שֶׁיָּצָא בּוֹ מֵהֶעֱלֶם לְגִלּוּי וְנִתְגַּלָּה עַצְמוּתוֹ בְּזֶה הַשֵּׁם ומ"שׁ \[וּמָה שֶׁנֶּאֱמַר\] 'בֵּינִי לְבֵין עַצְמִי' הוּא גִּלּוּיוֹ שֶׁבַּאֲצִילוּת שֶׁנִּתְגַּלָּה שָׁם מֵאֲמִתַּתוֹ הַנֶּעֱלָמָה שֶׁלְּמַעְלָה\]

**\[Meaning to say,** this is the Name **with which He emerged from concealment to revelation, and His Essence was revealed in this Name.** **And when it says ‘with Myself’ this refers to the radiance in *Atzilut* that was revealed there from the concealed True Being above**\***\]**

\*Note that the square brackets are in the original. The meaning of ‘stipulations with Myself’ will be explained shortly. 

The World of *Atzilut* (emanation) is the first of the immanent worlds. The word *Atzilut* in Hebrew derives from the word *etzel*, meaning “close to” or “near.”[^20] The world of *Atzilut* is “close to” its source, even though it is not united and identified with it to the same degree as the world of *Adam Kadmon*. Nevertheless it is a lofty and rarefied plane of reality which is merely emanated, not yet created. But the word *Atzilut* also means “to set aside,” “separated from,” as in the verse” And I will set aside from (*v’atzalti*) the spirit that is upon you and place it upon them”[^21] (Numbers 11:17), for the world of *Atzilut* is, so to speak, ‘set aside from’ God’s essence, as the Baal haLeshem goes on to explain.    

הוּא שְׁמִי שֶׁהִתְנֵיתִי בֵּינִי לְבֵין בְּרִיּוֹתַי ר"ל שֶׁנִּתְגַּלָּה עַל יָדוֹ לְזוּלָתוֹ וְהוּא בְּעוֹלָמוֹת בְּרִיאָה יְצִירָה וַעֲשִׂיָּה

The Midrash continues: **“And this is the Name with which I made stipulations between Myself and My creations,” meaning to say that there was revelation to others** i.e. to those who are not Him **by way of** the Name. This alludes to **the worlds of *Beriah, Yetizrah* and *Asiyah.***

*Beriah*, the world of Creation, is the second immanent world. It is a tremendous step down from the world of *Atzilut*, as it is the beginning of created being. ברא – the root of the word  *Beriah* (‘creation’), is always used in Hebrew to signify *yesh mi-ayin –* creation *ex nihilo*.[^22] This implies the coming into being of limited, restricted, finite existence. 

*Yetzirah* is the third of the four immanent spiritual worlds, the realm of spiritual existence in which the limited nature of the created beings takes on form and definition, and *Asiyah*, the fourth and lowest immanent worlds is the dimension where Godliness is most concealed. It is the final stage in the creative process and the lowest aspect of it includes the physical universe. The Baal haLeshem will explain all of these in their proper place (in *Shaar* 2).

וַהֲרֵי לָנוּ שֶׁכָּל הַגִּלּוּי דָּהַשֵׁם הויה ב"ה הוּא רַק מֵעֵת הָאֲצִילוּת וּלְהַלְאָה וְרַק מָה שֶׁנִּתְגַּלָּה בּוֹ לְזוּלָתוֹ (וְע' רע"מ פּ' בָּא מ"ב ב') 

**Thus we have it that all revelations** to us **of the blessed Name Havaye are exclusively from the moment** the world of ***Atzilut*** was emanated **and on, and only that which was revealed therein to those who are not Him.**

ומ"שׁ שֶׁהִתְנֵיתִי לָשׁוֹן תְּנַאי הוּא בְּמָה שֶׁגָּזְרָה רְצוֹנוֹ יִתְבָּרֵךְ לְהִתְגַּלָה רַק בַּגִּלּוּי הַזֶּה דַּוְקָא וְלֹא בְּאֹפֶן אַחֵר בְּשׁוּם פָּנִים וְכֵן לֹא לְמַטָּה מִמֶּנּוּ וְלֹא לְמַעְלָה מִמֶּנּוּ 

**And the phrase ‘with which I made stipulations’ expresses a condition** to be met **– signifying** that it depends **upon what His blessed will decrees – to reveal only in this specific manner of revelation and in no other way at all; and no lower nor loftier** than decreed. 

וְהוּא בְּמָה שֶׁנּוֹדַע שֶׁהַתְחַָלַת הַגִּלּוּי דְּהַשֵּׁם הויה ב"ה הוּא רַק בְּכָל הַנֶּאֱצָלִים אֲשֶׁר מֵהַצִּמְצוּם וּלְמַטָּה אֲבָל לֹא מֵהַצִּמְצוּם וּלְמַעְלָה

 Specifically, the decree is, **as is known, that the initial revelation of the blessed Name Havaye shall only be in that which is emanated after the *tzimtzum* and below, but not prior to the *tzimtzum*.**

וְכֵן גִּלּוּיוֹ לְזוּלָתוֹ הַנִּמְשָׁךְ מִמֶּנּוּ להבי"ע \[=לְהָעוֹלָמוֹת בְּרִיאָה, יְצִירָה וַעֲשִׂיָּה\] הִנֵּה הוּא בְּעוֹלַם הָאֲצִילוּת וְרַק בז"א \[=זְעֵיר אַנְפִּין\] וּמַלְכוּת וְלֹא יָצָא בָּעוֹלָמוֹת בי"ע בְּגִלּוּיָיו אֲשֶׁר לְמַעְלָה מהז"א וּמַלְכוּת

**In addition, the revelation to ‘something other’** i.e. to those levels below *Atzilut* that are regarded as ‘other’ **that is drawn down from the world of *Atzilut*** **to the worlds of *Beriah, Yetzirah and Asiyah*** abbreviated as *BiYA*), **is only** drawn **from the aspects of *zeir anpin* and *malchut*. Thus, the radiance that is drawn into the worlds of *BiYA*** (*Beriah, Yetzirah and Asiyah*) **is no higher than *zeir anpin* and *malchut*.**

*Ze’ir anpin* is a cluster of *sefirot* that includes *chesed, gevurah, tiferet, netzach, hod* and *yesod.* Its composition and function will be discussed later on. *Malchut* is the final *sefirah* and is also referred to as *Shechinah*. These will be explained at length in *shaar* 7. 

אֶלָּא רַק בְּהֲאוֹר דז"א וּמַלְכוּת לְבַד שֶׁהֵם הַשֵּׁמּוֹת דהויה אדני.

This radiance **is only from the light of *z”a*** (*ze’ir anpin*) **and *malchut*, which are the Names Havaye and Ad-nai**. 

וּכְנוֹדָע שֶׁעִקַּר הַגִּלּוּי דָּהֵשְׁם הויה ב"ה הוּא נִשְׁלַם בז"א וּמִתְלַבֵּשׁ בְּהֲמַּלְכוּת וְשָׂם נִתְגַּלָּה בְּהַשֵּׁם אדני

**As is known, the primary revelation of the blessed Name Havaye is**

**manifested completely in *z”a*, which is then clothed in *malchut*, where it is revealed as the Name Ad-nai. ** 

וְיָצָא עי"ז בְּהָעוֹלָמוֹת בי"ע וְלֹא הִמְשִׁיךְ גִּלּוּיוֹ דְּעֲצְמוּת אוֹר הַשֵּׁם הויה ב"ה יוֹתֵר לְמַטָּה שֶׁהוּא בְּעוֹלָם הַבְּרִיאָה

**By way of this** process **it descends into the worlds of *BiYA*.** However, **the radiance of the essence of the blessed Name Havaye is not drawn down to the World of Beriah**

וּכְנוֹדֵעַ שֶׁעִקַּר עֲצְמוּת אֱלָהוּת נִתְצַמְּצֵם לְהִסְתַּיֵּם בַּסּוֹף הֲמַּלְכוּת דַּאֲצִילוּת שֶׁהִיא הָה' אַחֲרוֹנָה דָּהַשֵּׁם וְהִיא הַמִּתְגַּלָּה בְּשֵׁם אדנ"י 

**As is known, the primary essence of Godliness** i.e. divine revelation **is** **curtailed and terminates at the lowest level of *malchut* of *Atzilut,* corresponding to the final** letter ***hai*** **of the Name Havaye, and is revealed as the Name Ad-nai.**  

וְנִתְצַמְצֵם וּמִסְתַּיֵּם עֲצְמוּת אֱלָהוּת בַּסּוֹף הֲמַּלְכוּת דְּאֲצִילוּת וּמִשָּׁם וּלְמַטָה הֵם נִבְרָאִים נוֹצָרִים נַעֲשִׂים שֶׁאֵינָם אֱלָהוּת כְּלָל 

As mentioned **the essence of Godliness** **is curtailed and terminates at the lowest level of *malchut* of *Atzilut*. From there on below are the created, formed and made beings** in the worlds of *Beriah, Yetzirah and Asiyah* respectively, **that are not Godliness at all.**

וְכָל אוֹרוֹ הַנִּמְשָׁךְ בָּהֶם הוּא רַק ע"י מְסָכִים

**All the light drawn into them is only via ‘screens.’** Light on the other side of a screen is called *“ohr shel toldah*” – second-generation or derivative light, not original first-generation light.[^23]    

וְלֹא נִמְשַׁךְ בָּהֶם אֶלָּא רַק תּוֹלְדוֹת הֱאָרָה לְבַד וּבַזֶה בָּרָא וְיָצַר וְעָשָׂה כָּל הַמְּצִיאוּת דהבי"ע 

**There is not drawn into them** the worlds of *BiYA* **anything other than a mere radiance, and it was from this that He created, formed and made the worlds of *BiYA* as they exist.**

\[וּכְמוֹ שֶׁמְּבֹאַר בְּסֵדֶר אֲצִילוּת בְּקִצּוּר פ"א וּבִדְרוּשִׁי אבי"ע פ' י"ג וּבְסֵדֶר אבי''ע ספ"א \[=סוֹף פֶּרֶק א'\] וּבְכִסֵּא הַכָּבוֹד ספ"א. וְע' סֵדֶר אבי"ע פ"ו וכסה"כ פ"ה. וּכְנוֹדַע כ"ז \[=כָּל זֶה\] לְכָל טוֹעֲמֵי טַעַם עֵץ הֲחַיִּים\]

**\[All of this is explained in** *Etz Chaim*, **in Seder Atzilut in chap. 1 summary; ibid. Drushei ABiYA chap 13; Seder ABiYA chap. 1 (end); ibid *Kisei Hakavod* chap. 1 (end). See also Seder ABiYA chap. 6; *Kisei Hakavod* chap. 5\. As is known to those who have tasted from the *Etz Chaim*** The Tree of Life i.e. the Kabbalah of the Arizal**\].**

The brackets appear in the original. This is a parenthetical statement offering some sources for further study.   

וְזֶהוּ שֶׁאָמְרוּ הִתְנֵיתִי לְשׁוֹן תְּנַאי לוֹמַר שֶׁלֹּא נִתְגַּלָּה אֶלָּא בַּגִּלּוּי הַזֶּה דַּוְקָא וְלֹא נִתָּן רְשׁוּת לִקְרוֹתוֹ וּלְעַבְּדוֹ אֶלָּא רַק בְּאוֹתוֹ הַגִּלּוּי דַּוְקָא:

To return to our earlier theme: **this is why the Sages said that** the word **הִתְנֵיתִי** (I stipulated) **expresses a condition** to be met **– for He would not be revealed other than in this manner, and permission was not given to call out to Him and serve Him other than via this exact revelation.**
