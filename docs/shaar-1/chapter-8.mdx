[^1]: Cf. *Isaiah* 55:8 — כִּי לֹא מַחְשְׁבוֹתַי מַחְשְׁבוֹתֵיכֶם וְלֹא דַרְכֵיכֶם דְּרָכַי נְאֻם ה׳.

[^2]: Cf. *Pirkei Avot* 1:17 — וְלֹא הַמִּדְרָשׁ הוּא הָעִקָּר, אֶלָּא הַמַּעֲשֶׂה.

[^3]: This seems to be a reference to the *sefirot* of the world of Atzilut.

[^4]: This is a point of disagreement between the Ramak and the Arizal. The latter maintained that even the lofty world of Atzilut is emanated only as a result of the *tzimtzum*, making a radical distinction between the Creator and the created. The Ramak did not know about the concept of *tzimtzum* in this sense. However, the Arizal agrees that the *tzimtzum* did not change anything in G-d Himself; only that His Infinite Light was concealed from creation.

[^5]: הָבֶל can be translated as breath, mist, or steam. But it also carries the meaning of futility or inconsequentiality. See *Kohelet* 1:2 — הֲבֵל הֲבָלִים אָמַר קֹהֶלֶת, הֲבֵל הֲבָלִים הַכֹּל הָבֶל.

[^6]: See above 1:7: In order for the light to become invested in the finite worlds without nullifying them, even after the *tzimtzum*, it must pass through various 'screens' that attenuate and diminish the light, as the Arizal explains in *Etz Chaim shaar* 44, chap. 11.

[^7]: Cf. *Isaiah* 40:5 — וְנִגְלָה כְּבוֹד יְהוָה וְרָאוּ כָל בָּשָׂר יַחְדָּו כִּי פִּי יְהוָה דִּבֵּר.
