**The Importance of the Inner Dimensions of Torah**

<Passage

  translation="And all the more so when a person occupies himself down here with the inner dimensions of the Torah — i.e. with Kabbalah — in matters pertaining to the light of His holiness itself in Atzilut, that transcends the 'cloaking' that is prevalent in the worlds of *BiYA*."
>

As mentioned earlier, in order for the light to descend to *BiYA* it must be dimmed and attenuated. This process is akin to covering or cloaking it in garments which obscure the light.

</Passage>

<Passage
  marker="§ 9:12"
  hebrew="וְהוּא עַל דֶּרֶךְ שֶׁאָנוּ רוֹאִין שֶׁיֵּשׁ חִלּוּק בַּדִּין בֵּין הַכּוֹתֵב אֶת הַשֵּׁמוֹת הַקְּדוֹשִׁים לִשְׁאָר כָּל הַתּוֹרָה כִּי הַשֵּׁמוֹת צָרִיךְ לְקַדְּשָׁם בִּקְדֻשָּׁה כְּפוּלָה נוֹסָף עַל קְדֻשַּׁת שְׁאָר כָּל הַתּוֹרָה"
  translation="This is analogous to the difference we see in Jewish law between writing the holy Names and writing all the other words of the Torah: the holy Names require sanctification with double sanctity over and above the sanctity of the rest of the Torah.[^4]"
>

For example, before a scribe writes any words of the Torah, he declares, "I am hereby writing this Torah scroll keeping in mind the sanctity of the Torah." Nevertheless, whenever he writes one of the holy Names he must first declare, "I am hereby writing this holy Name for the sanctity of the holy Name."[^5] From this we see the Names have a higher level of sanctity. In addition, while the scribe is writing one of the Names, he may not interrupt, even if a Jewish king addresses him. Furthermore, the Names may not be erased, not even a single letter.

</Passage>

[^1]: Rabbi Isaiah Horowitz (1565–1630). His nickname *Shelah* is taken from the initials of his magnum opus *Shnei Luchot HaBrit.*

[^2]: Rabbi Chaim of Volozhin (1749–1821).

[^3]: The verse states, "If you will follow My decrees and perform them, then I will provide your rain in their time…" (Leviticus 26:3-4). However the word for *them* ('perform them') is written in the defective form so that it can also be read as 'you will make them' — you are the ones who establish them above.

[^4]: *Shulchan Aruch, Yoreh De'ah* 276:2.

[^5]: *Berachot* 32b; *Shulchan Aruch, Orach Chaim* chapter 104.

[^6]: Ramban, Intro. to *Bereishit*.

[^7]: Rabbi Moshe ben Menachem of Prague (1650–circa 1710).

[^8]: It seems that the Baal HaLeshem is referring to the book *Etz Chaim* here (which was compiled from Rabbi Chaim Vital's manuscripts by Rabbi Meir Poppers) rather than the generic term *Etz HaChaim* used for the entire collection known as the *Shemoneh She'arim*.

[^9]: Cf. *Chabbakuk* 3:4.

[^10]: A Kabbalistic prayer book based on the Arizal's teachings, written by Rabbi Nathan Nata ben Moshe Hannover Ashkenazi (d. 1683), a student of the Maharsha and of Rabbi Shimshon of Ostropol.

[^11]: The standard version in *Shaarei Tziyon, Sha'ar* 3. The version here may be a misprint, particularly in light of the context.

[^12]: The proper translation of נִשְׁתַּלְשְׁלוּ should be 'devolved,' i.e. in a regressive sense, since things 'devolve' from a higher spiritual status to a lower one.
